# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Observant Muslim women 25-45 and high-income luxury fashion buyers who value modesty. Fashion-literate, globally-minded, urban — they benchmark NÜRHAUS against The Row or Bottega Veneta, not generic abaya shops. They are buying a considered object, not a trend. Context: shopping on a phone between meetings or on a laptop in the evening, unhurried.

## Product Purpose

Launch collection page for a modest luxury fashion brand. Eight pieces (two abayas, six dresses) at premium price points ($390-$720). Primary goals: establish brand authority as editorial, not commercial; convert first-time visitors into buyers or list subscribers; build a long-term relationship with a discerning customer.

## Brand Personality

Editorial. Sharp. Quiet authority. Says less, means more. Measured — never hurried, never loud.

## Anti-references

- Generic abaya shops: busy product grids, discount banners, bright colours, cluttered navigation
- Western luxury clichés (script logos, black-and-white with gold): too familiar, not distinctive
- Minimal SaaS aesthetic: clean but soulless, no cultural weight

## Design Principles

1. The garment is the message — never out-decorate the product
2. Silence between elements is as deliberate as the elements themselves
3. Editorial confidence: strong hierarchy, sparse copy, nothing needs explaining twice
4. Restraint earns attention — every decoration must justify itself
5. Cultural depth without exoticism — the Arabic script and mihrab arch are structural, not ornamental

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Reduced-motion respected (already coded). Arabic text rendered with Amiri; `dir="rtl"` applied at element level.
